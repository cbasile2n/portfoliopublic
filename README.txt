Christian Daniel Basile
Computational scientist working at the intersection of machine learning, quantitative modeling, and biological systems.
My background combines machine learning, data science, quantitative systems pharmacology (QSP), and mechanistic modeling, with experience across healthcare analytics, computational biology, and applied research. I focus on building interpretable models that move beyond prediction—frameworks that help explain why systems behave the way they do and how those insights can support better decisions.
My work spans ODE-based systems modeling, machine learning workflows, optimization, and translational applications in health and life sciences. More recently, I’ve been exploring neuroimmune mechanisms, brain-related system dynamics, and computational approaches that translate clinical observations into testable biological hypotheses.
I’m particularly interested in problems where rigorous modeling can create practical impact—whether in drug response, healthcare systems, biological complexity, or applied AI.
